        Important Notes
        About This Tool ▾

        This tool helps art and design academics and HDR candidates prepare for conversations with their Academic Supervisor or Deputy Head of School/Director (Research) about whether their creative work has research dimensions.

        Important: Your responses guide discussion — they don't determine outcomes. Your supervisor makes final classifications in consultation with you. This tool assists in determining if your creative work should be uploaded to the Griffith University Creative Works repository or recorded using other categories in Symplectic Elements.

        Based on ERA (Excellence in Research for Australia) frameworks. Work that does not meet research criteria may still constitute significant disciplinary scholarship or professional practice, both of which are recorded in Symplectic Elements as non-research creative outputs and are valued parts of your academic profile.

        Tool Development

        Developed by Rebekah Davis, Griffith University, using Claude (Anthropic) as a research and development assistant. Reflects current Australian research assessment frameworks and institutional best practices.
